# Style Guide

## Default Positioning

Use a viewpoint-insight account voice: sharp enough to have a stance, but not exaggerated. Prefer practical judgment over hype.

## Copy Rules

- Title: make a clear claim in 16-28 Chinese characters when possible.
- Opening: state the counterintuitive observation first.
- Body: use short paragraphs, one idea per paragraph.
- Lists: use ordered lists for logic, bullets for examples.
- Tone: default to restrained and professional.
- Tags: include 3-6 relevant tags at the end.
- Close: ask one concrete discussion question.

## Optional Tone Overrides

- `更种草`: more benefit-oriented, but do not promise income or outcomes.
- `更犀利`: stronger contrast and judgment, but avoid attacks or absolutist claims.
- `更教程`: more procedural, with steps and checklists.

## Risk Rewrite Heuristics

- Replace "一定/必然/所有人" with "更可能/很多情况下/一部分人".
- Replace income promises with non-guaranteed capability or workflow benefits.
- Add attribution or soften claims that sound like verified facts.
- Avoid sensational platform-risk wording when calmer phrasing works.

## Image Placeholder Pattern

Use exactly three placeholders unless the user asks otherwise:

```text
[图1: 封面 | 核心钩子: ...]
[图2: 核心观点 | 三个判断: ...]
[图3: 总结行动 | 建议清单: ...]
```

## Card Visual Theme

Default to the confirmed Xiaohongshu reference-inspired theme:

- Cover: grid-paper background, thick black border, oversized black title, white text shadow, neon green highlight, small black/green badges, and each hook clause as a separate numbered point.
- Core viewpoint: dark blue-purple glass card, white high-contrast typography, translucent point containers, and one short hook such as `3 个判断，一图看懂`.
- Summary action: light grid-paper background, black outline cards, slight rotation for handwritten note energy, bright blue/orange accent labels.

Keep cards readable at mobile feed size:

- Use short card text; visual points should usually be 6-15 Chinese characters.
- Split long image sentences into independent points. Treat `;`, `；`, `,`, `，`, `、`, and line breaks as point separators.
- Do not repeat a full long placeholder sentence as a hook. Use a short summary hook, then render the clauses as separate visual points.
- Prefer strong contrast and plain backgrounds over decorative gradients.
- Avoid dense paragraphs inside images unless the user explicitly asks for a text-heavy note page.
